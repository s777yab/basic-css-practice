Eiichiro Oda

The best storyteller to ever live!

Eiichiro Oda is a Japanese manga artist and the creator of the series One Piece.

Here's a time line of Eiichiro Oda's life:

  • 1975: Eiichiro Oda is born in Kumamoto City, Kumamoto Prefecture, Japan.
  • 1992: Oda wins the Tezuka Award for his one-shot manga "Wanted!"
  • 1994: Oda assists manga artist Shinobu Kaitani on the series "Suizan Police Gang."
  • 1996: Oda's one-shot manga "Romance Dawn" is published, which later serves as a prototype for "One Piece."
  • 1997: Eiichiro Oda begins serializing "One Piece" in Shueisha's Weekly Shonen Jump magazine.
  • 1999: Oda marries Chiaki Inaba.
  • 2003: The "One Piece" anime adaptation begins airing on television.
  • 2008: "One Piece" reaches its 500th chapter milestone.
  • 2010: "One Piece" celebrates its 10th anniversary as an anime.
  • 2012: "One Piece" reaches its 600th chapter milestone.
  • 2014: "One Piece" celebrates its 15th anniversary as an anime.
  • 2017: "One Piece" reaches its 800th chapter milestone.
  • 2019: "One Piece" celebrates its 20th anniversary as an anime.
  • 2020: "One Piece" reaches its 1000th chapter milestone.

Oda continues to work on "One Piece," and the series maintains its status as one of the most successful and influential manga of all time.
